Background 
Risk is part of everyday life, and how people navigate potential jackpots and disasters can have major consequences for individual and societal well-being. A major source of risk comes from other humans: people can be loyal friends, but also bitter rivals. One response to this interpersonal risk can be seen in a quickly-growing security industry, where people and industries are increasing spending on security cameras, guards, heavy-duty locks, and firewalls. However, little is understood about how people choose whether to expose or protect themselves from the actions of others.
